PUBLIC EXPENSES

Brazilian congresspeople are paying for dozens of meals in the same day

BY Luiz Fernando Toledo

Photo: Zeca Ribeiro/Banco de Imagens da Câmara dos Deputados

Brazilian congresspeople are famous for spending money on fancy food and restaurants.

They have the right to use a certain amount of public funds every year for mandate expenses, including food. Congress rules declare that this money, known as “cota parlamentar”, should be used to pay for congresspeople’s own meals.

But many more meals are being pade in one single receipt. In a case, a representative had 7 omelettes, 3 stout beers and a bottle of wine. Another person had 49 lunches in a single day.

An analysis made using open data shows that dozens of meals have been paid by congresspeople who took office in 2019 on the same day and receipt. Some representatives have spent over R$ 3,000 in just one meal (US$ 565) with over 30 plates. The rules state that the receipts should be presented by the politician in order to get reimbursed.

Public data can show how much money was spent and the the place where the money was spent, but it doesn't always reveal what was the food neither who were with them. The Congress rules don’t require representatives to justify these expenses.

Summarizing the expenses

First let's take a look on how much federal deputies spent in 2019, 2020 and 2021, after there were elected, individually. This is the sum of all their receipts. I chose to analyze the data from different years together because 2020 and 2021 were very atypical years due to the pandemic and the home office policy in the Congress. Less money was spent in food, transportation, etc.
