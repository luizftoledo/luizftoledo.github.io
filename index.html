Data and investigative journalism

I am Luiz Fernando Toledo, a journalist using investigative and data techniques to bring powerful stories to life.


Here's what I do


I am currently pursuing a master's degree in data journalism at Columbia University after being awarded a full scholarship due to professional achievements in my home country, Brazil.

Read my full resume


I can report both in Portuguese and English, cover breaking news at a fast pace, do research on national or cross-border journalistic projects, analyze huge databases using programming languages ( Python, SQL), automate journalistic tasks (BeautifulSoup, Selenium - see my FOIA scraper below), get documents using Freedom of Information requests and open records. (I wrote a guide on how to use it).

I have also coordinated and/or contributed to multiple projects that improve government transparency. I co-founded Fiquem Sabendo, a Brazilian NGO that finds, organizes and releases relevant public data for free. Our work and expertise is being used as a source of data by every major news outlet in Brazil, both at local, national and international levels. The project is a member of the Global Investigative Journalism Network (GIJN) and received funds from Google News Initiative in 2021.

I am also one of the Brazilian Association for Investigative Journalism (Abraji) directors, one of the world's leading associations of investigative reporters, and develop different projects there to help journalists get and use public data. One of these projects was awarded a six-month fellowship to study at the University of Oxford and became a national training on public records for thousands of journalists.
