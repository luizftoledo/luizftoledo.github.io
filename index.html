Data and investigative journalism

I am Luiz Fernando Toledo, a journalist using investigative and data techniques to write stories that impact society positively.


I am currently pursuing a master's degree in data journalism at Columbia University after being awarded a full scholarship due to professional achievements in my home country, Brazil.

Skills

I can report both in Portuguese and English, cover breaking news under tight deadlines, collaborate with or coordinate national or cross-border journalistic projects, analyze huge databases using programming languages ( Python, SQL), automate journalistic tasks (see FOIA scraper below), get documents using different techniques, from sourcing to Freedom of Information requests, and do academic research.

Independent work

I have also coordinated and contributed to multiple projects that improve government transparency. I co-founded Fiquem Sabendo, a Brazilian NGO that finds, organizes and releases relevant public data for free. Our work and expertise is being used as a source of data by every major news outlet in Brazil, both at local, national and international levels. The project is a member of the Global Investigative Journalism Network (GIJN) and received funds from Google News Initiative in 2021. I am also one of the Brazilian Association for Investigative Journalism (Abraji) directors, one of the world's leading associations of investigative reporters, and develop different projects there to help journalists get and use public data.

Scraping every Brazil's Freedom of Informaction Act (FOIA) requests (Columbia Journalism School)

I have created two scrapers that monitor FOIA requests (Freedom of Information Act or Lei de Acesso à Informação in Portuguese) sent by Brazilian citizens in a spreadsheet based on a specific keyword.
